# Research And Discovery

## Purpose

The research stage prevents Product Pipeline from turning intuition into a backlog too early.

Research should gather enough evidence to sharpen the product direction, identify risks, and make clear what should be validated with Richard before work is sliced.

## Discovery Inputs

- Human product idea and desired outcome.
- Target customer or user guess.
- Pain points, frustrations, or inefficient workflows.
- Known constraints from the parent KB.
- AppGenerator or base-shell capability assumptions.
- Existing competitive or analogous products.
- Any hard business, compliance, platform, or timeline constraints.

## Research Outputs

- Market or workflow map.
- Alternative and competitor notes.
- Target segment profile.
- Differentiator thesis.
- Emotional-calling statement.
- Risk and unknowns list.
- Recommended first wedge.
- Evidence links or source notes when external research is used.

## Research Quality Bar

Research is useful when it changes or confirms what should be built first. It is weak when it only summarizes generic market facts.

Good research answers:

- Who feels this pain most sharply?
- What do they use today?
- What is unsatisfying about current options?
- What first wedge can prove value quickly?
- What claims are uncertain enough to ask the human before creating tasks?

## Agent Rules

- Separate facts from inference.
- Mark assumptions explicitly.
- Prefer comparative evidence over isolated feature lists.
- Do not browse or cite unstable claims without checking current sources.
- Do not create ready-for-dev tasks from unsupported research.
