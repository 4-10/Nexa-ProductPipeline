# Product Strategy

## What This Is

Strategy knowledge for why Product Pipeline exists, what pain it solves, and how it should help Nexa move faster without losing product judgment.

## Strategic Goal

Product Pipeline should prevent the backlog from running dry when implementation velocity increases. It should turn a human's high-level idea into enough researched, well-shaped, ready work that build agents can keep moving without inventing product direction on their own.

## Product Thesis

Nexa needs a product operating layer, not just a build system. As AppGenerator makes app creation faster, the scarce resource becomes clear product direction: who the product serves, why it matters, what differentiates it, what to build first, and what should wait.

Product Pipeline should make that direction durable.

## Near-Term Objective

Design the v0 module workflow and artifacts:

- intake conversation,
- research stage,
- comparative analysis,
- target market focus,
- differentiator and emotional-calling synthesis,
- epic/story/task generation,
- GitHub issue/project creation,
- parent KB updates and audit trail.

## Product Principles

- Human intent stays in control; agents refine and structure it.
- Research precedes confident backlog creation.
- Epics and tasks must include non-goals and verification expectations.
- The module should optimize for usable build work, not product theater.
- Emotional resonance matters because product direction is not only functional.
- The final module should integrate with the parent KB instead of creating isolated planning memory.

## Risks

- Creating generic backlog filler that looks productive but does not encode strategy.
- Over-automating product judgment before the research and questioning loop is proven.
- Merging design responsibilities too early and muddying module boundaries.
- Generating tasks that are too broad, too vague, or missing ready-for-dev evidence.
- Letting the temporary build KB be mistaken for the final module architecture.
